Anatomy

Almost all guitar pickups can be broken down into three basic parts:
Magents: pole pieces, bar magnets, or both.
Coiled Wire: conductive wire wrapped around a part of the pickup.
Housing: the structure that houses the pickup.
These parts come together to form an electromagnetic device that forms a magnet current
that magnetizes the guitar string and "picks up" the vibrations to amplify the sound.
There are a variety of different types of guitar pickups, each built different to change the sound of the pickup.
